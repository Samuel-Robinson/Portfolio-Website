Speculative Design for Inclusion and Diversity

Several speculative design interventions for the purpose of a more inclusionary and accessible world.

April, 2021 | 3 min read

Project Framing

Throughout the duration of my Trends in UX university course, I was tasked with imagining, designing, and iterating three separate speculative projects. The first was a brand new elevator system, that removes long wait times, and provides a more accessible experience for all. The second, is a DSLR camera remote, that allows more people to enjoy the wonderful joys of photography. Finally, through synthesizing the two designs, I created an elevator control panel that combined the best of both designs.

Each of these projects addresses different problems, and aims to remedy this problems through design interventions.

The remote on the other hand aims to provide larger buttons, easier controls, and a simple and fun experience for many more.

Conclusion

Finally, the elevator control takes the ideas of both the fix the problem of regular and difficult to use elevator controls. It includes audio, visual, and even haptic cues to alert elevator users as to location and it can reach out from the wall for those who are unable to reach normally. It also includes larger buttons and cancel options when the wrong button is pressed.

Overall, the solutions are not perfect and would benefit from more user research and testing. Still, they are a step in the right direction to creating a more inclusionary and accessible world. It is hoped that these projects might serve as future inspiration as I continue to expand my knowledge in this area going forward.
